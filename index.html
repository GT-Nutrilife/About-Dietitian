About Me

Where Science Meets Compassion

Good nutrition shouldn't feel complicated. Whether it's a patient recovering from surgery, a teenager who's never thought about vegetables, or a parent trying to feed their family well on a budget — my job is to meet people where they are and help them make better food choices.

My background includes hospital dietetics, research on adolescent eating habits (funded by the International Food Policy Research Institute), and community health projects across rural Sri Lanka.

I graduated with Second Class Upper Division from Wayamba University, where I also served as Vice President of the Nutrition Society.

How Do Rural Teenagers in Anuradhapura Eat Fruits and Vegetables?

For my undergraduate thesis, I looked at what makes teenagers in rural Anuradhapura reach for — or avoid — fruits and vegetables. What influences their choices? What gets in the way?

The project was funded by the International Food Policy Research Institute (IFPRI). I was selected to present my findings at the Delivering for Nutrition in South Asia 2024 Conference.
